US Code Title 17 - Copyright Law
"Copyright protection subsists explicitly in original works of authorship fixed in any tangible medium of expression. This includes literary, musical, dramatic, and choreographic works. The owner of copyright has the exclusive right to reproduce the work, prepare derivative works, and distribute copies."

State Contract Law - General Principles
"A legally binding contract requires an offer, acceptance, and consideration. Consideration is the exchange of something of value between the parties. Contracts can be written or oral, but certain types (like real estate sales) must be in writing to be enforceable under the Statute of Frauds."

India Legal Process Note - FIR and Police Complaint
"If police refuse to register an FIR, a person may keep written records, submit a written complaint to a senior police officer, and consider approaching the appropriate magistrate or legal aid service. Procedure depends on jurisdiction and facts."

Consumer Dispute Note - Security Deposit and Service Complaints
"For deposit or consumer disputes, useful records include the agreement, payment proof, written communication, photographs, notices, and a dated timeline. Many cases start with a written demand or legal notice before formal complaint filing."

Employment Law Note - Salary and Termination Records
"For salary, wrongful termination, or workplace disputes, preserve appointment letters, payslips, emails, attendance records, termination letters, and messages. Local labor law, contract terms, and limitation periods affect next steps."

Safety and Legal Aid Note - Domestic Violence or Threats
"When there is immediate violence or threat, personal safety comes before paperwork. Contact emergency services, a trusted person, shelter, legal aid, or a qualified local lawyer for protection and jurisdiction-specific remedies."
