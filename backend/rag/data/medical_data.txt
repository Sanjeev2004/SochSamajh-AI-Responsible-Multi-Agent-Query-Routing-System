Dr. John Smith, M.D. - Type 2 Diabetes Management
"Type 2 diabetes is a chronic condition affecting the way the body processes blood sugar (glucose). Symptoms include increased thirst, frequent urination, hunger, fatigue, and blurred vision. Treatment often involves lifestyle changes such as diet and exercise, and potentially medication like Metformin or insulin therapy."

Dr. Emily Chen, M.D. - Migraine and Headaches
"Migraines are severe headaches often accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound. Triggers can include stress, certain foods, and hormonal changes. Over-the-counter pain relievers (NSAIDs) can help, but prescription triptans are often needed for severe cases. Rest in a dark, quiet room is recommended."

Emergency Triage Reference - Chest Pain and Breathing Difficulty
"Chest pain with sweating, left arm pain, fainting, shortness of breath, blue lips, confusion, or severe weakness can be a medical emergency. People with these symptoms should seek emergency care immediately and should not drive themselves."

Public Health Note - Dog Bite and Rabies Risk
"Dog bites should be cleaned with running water and soap, and urgent medical evaluation is important when vaccine status is unknown. Rabies post-exposure prophylaxis is time-sensitive and should be assessed by a healthcare professional."

Maternal Health Safety Note - Pregnancy Bleeding
"Bleeding during pregnancy, severe abdominal pain, fainting, fever, or reduced fetal movement should be assessed urgently by a qualified healthcare professional or emergency department."

Infectious Disease Note - Dengue Warning Signs
"Dengue can cause fever, severe body pain, headache, rash, nausea, and weakness. Warning signs include severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, bleeding, lethargy, restlessness, and difficulty breathing; these need urgent medical care."
